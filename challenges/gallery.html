The experimental clock I designed uses abstract shapes—circles, squares, and triangles—to represent hours, minutes, and seconds. Instead of traditional clock hands, these shapes orbit and rotate in response to mouse movement, creating a dynamic, interactive experience. The fluid transitions and pulsating shapes give the clock a "trippy" feel, making time appear kinetic and ever-changing.
